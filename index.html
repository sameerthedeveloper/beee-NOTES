BEEE Semester Study Notes

Module 1: Basic Circuit Laws and Analysis

  • Ohm's Law: \( V = IR \), where \( V \) = voltage, \( I \) = current, \( R \) = resistance.
  • Resistance Formula: \( R = \frac{\rho l}{A} \) where \( \rho \) = resistivity, \( l \) = length, \( A \) = cross-sectional area.
  • Series Circuits: Total resistance \( R_{eq} = R_1 + R_2 + \ldots + R_n \).
  • Parallel Circuits: \( \frac{1}{R_{eq}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + \ldots + \frac{1}{R_n} \).
  • Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL): Sum of voltages around any closed loop is zero: \( \sum V = 0 \).
  • Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL): Sum of currents entering a junction equals sum leaving: \( \sum I = 0 \).
  • Mesh Analysis: Apply KVL to find mesh currents in complex circuits.
  • Nodal Analysis: Use KCL to find node voltages, currents expressed as \( I = \frac{V}{R} \).
  • Star-Delta Transformation: Convert between star (Y) and delta (Δ) resistor networks using standard formulas to simplify circuit analysis.

Module 2: AC Circuits

  • Alternating Current (AC): Current that reverses direction periodically; represented by sinusoidal functions.
  • Impedance (Z): \( Z = R + jX \), total opposition to AC, with resistance \( R \) and reactance \( X \).
  • Reactance: \( X_L = 2\pi f L \) for inductors, \( X_C = \frac{1}{2\pi f C} \) for capacitors.
  • RMS Values: \( V_{rms} = \frac{V_{peak}}{\sqrt{2}} \), \( I_{rms} = \frac{I_{peak}}{\sqrt{2}} \).
  • RL Circuit: Voltage \( V = IR + L \frac{dI}{dt} \), current response: \( I(t) = \frac{V}{R} (1 - e^{-\frac{R}{L}t}) \).
  • RC Circuit: Voltage \( V = IR + \frac{1}{C} \int I dt \), current response: \( I(t) = \frac{V}{R} e^{-t/RC} \).
  • RLC Circuit: Second-order differential equation: \( L \frac{d^2 I}{dt^2} + R \frac{dI}{dt} + \frac{1}{C} I = \frac{dV}{dt} \).
  • Power Factor (PF): \( \cos \theta = \frac{R}{Z} \), ratio of real power to apparent power.
  • Resonance: Occurs when \( X_L = X_C \), circuit behaves purely resistive.

Module 3: Electrical Machines

  • DC Generator EMF: \( E = \frac{P \Phi Z N}{60 A} \), where \( P \) = number of poles, \( \Phi \) = flux per pole, \( Z \) = total conductors, \( N \) = speed (rpm), \( A \) = parallel paths.
  • DC Motor Back EMF: \( E_b = \frac{P \Phi Z N}{60 A} \), opposes applied voltage and controls speed.
  • Torque in DC Motor: \( T \propto \Phi I_a \), proportional to flux and armature current.
  • Transformer EMF Equation: \( E = 4.44 f N \Phi_m \), where \( f \) = frequency, \( N \) = number of turns, \( \Phi_m \) = max flux.
  • Transformer Turns Ratio: \( \frac{E_1}{E_2} = \frac{N_1}{N_2} \), relates primary and secondary voltages to turns.
  • Efficiency and Regulation: Efficiency = output/input × 100%; Regulation indicates voltage change from no load to full load.

Module 4: Semiconductor Devices

  • PN Junction Diode: Allows current flow in one direction; Shockley diode equation: \( I = I_0 (e^{\frac{V}{\eta V_T}} - 1) \) where \( V_T \) is thermal voltage.
  • Zener Diode: Operates in reverse breakdown region, used for voltage regulation and reference.
  • Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT): Current controlled device; \( I_C = \beta I_B \) with modes: active, saturation, cut-off.
  • Field Effect Transistor (FET): Voltage controlled device with high input impedance.
  • Half-wave and Full-wave Rectifiers: Convert AC to DC; full-wave has higher efficiency and less ripple.
  • Filters: Capacitor and inductor filters used to smooth rectified output.

Module 5: Digital Electronics

  • Logic Gates: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, XNOR basic gates.
  • SR Flip-Flop: \( Q_{next} = S + \overline{R}Q \); basic bistable storage element.
  • JK Flip-Flop: \( Q_{next} = J\overline{Q} + \overline{K}Q \); resolves invalid SR states.
  • D Flip-Flop: Data latch; output follows input on clock edge.
  • Half Adder: Sum = \( A \oplus B \), Carry = \( AB \).
  • Full Adder: Sum = \( A \oplus B \oplus C_{in} \), Carry = \( AB + BC_{in} + AC_{in} \).
  • Multiplexers and Demultiplexers: Data selectors and routing devices.
  • A/D Converters: Flash comparator and successive approximation types.
  • D/A Converters: R-2R ladder and weighted resistor types.
  • Shift Registers and Counters: Sequential logic elements used for data storage and counting.
